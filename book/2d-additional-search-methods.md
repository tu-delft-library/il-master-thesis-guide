# 2d. Additional Search Methods
## Introduction
When you have set up a good keyword search strategy, it can be helpful to add some additional ways of searching to your strategy to help you find more literature. We recommend you use these techniques as an addition to your keyword search and not as a replacement. This is because a keyword search is the most rigorous way to find the largest amount of relevant literature.

Techniques you could use in addition to keyword searching are: Snowballing, Similar Document Searching and AI Searching

## Step 1: Snowballing

Snowballing means finding more results based on the citations of highly relevant publications. There are two types of snowballing: When _forward snowballing_, you can find other relevant articles by looking at which publications cite a specific key publication. 

In _backward snowballing_, you take a very recent key article and find more relevant results by looking at the references made by this key article.

## Step 3: AI Searching

In addition to citation searching, there are new AI-assisted search techniques emerging alongside traditional keyword-searching that can help you find additional information. Academic tools like Elicit and SciSpace use semantic searching to find additional papers. The tools search semantic scholar and retrieve articles where the language used best matches the language of a query written in natural language. 

This technology can be used to find papers in addition to those found through a keyword search. You can use these tools by simply putting in your research question and ask it to find relevant peer-reviewed articles. 

A lot of these tools also offer possibilities to build further on the information you found by connecting it to citations or similar results.
